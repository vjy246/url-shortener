# URL Shortener Service - Finance/Budget Owner Interview Questions

## Overview
This document contains preliminary interview questions for Finance and Budget Owner stakeholders. These questions focus on financial planning, budgeting, ROI, cost management, and resource allocation.

---

## 1. Budget & Financial Planning

### 1.1 Overall Budget
- **Q1.1.1**: Total budget allocated for URL shortener service?
- **Q1.1.2**: Budget breakdown by category?
  - Development/engineering
  - Infrastructure/hosting
  - Operations/support
  - Marketing/sales
  - Tools/licenses
- **Q1.1.3**: Budget cycle? (monthly, quarterly, annual review)
- **Q1.1.4**: Is budget fixed or flexible based on performance?
- **Q1.1.5**: What's the payback period expectation?
- **Q1.1.6**: Budget allocation for future years? (Year 1, Year 2, Year 3)

### 1.2 Development Costs
- **Q1.2.1**: Engineering headcount allocated? (how many FTEs)
- **Q1.2.2**: Expected development timeline? (months to MVP, to v1.0)
- **Q1.2.3**: Engineering cost per month? (salary budget, benefits)
- **Q1.2.4**: Infrastructure/tools costs for development?
  - CI/CD pipelines
  - Monitoring/observability tools
  - Testing tools
  - Version control, communication tools
- **Q1.2.5**: Third-party services/licenses needed? (APIs, databases, frameworks)
- **Q1.2.6**: External consultants/contractors needed? (budget)

### 1.3 Infrastructure Costs
- **Q1.3.1**: Cloud infrastructure budget? (AWS, Azure, GCP - monthly estimate)
- **Q1.3.2**: Expected infrastructure cost at different scales?
  - At launch
  - 10x growth
  - 100x growth
- **Q1.3.3**: Data storage costs? (database, logging, backups, archives)
- **Q1.3.4**: CDN/egress costs?
- **Q1.3.5**: Third-party services costs? (analytics, monitoring, payments)
- **Q1.3.6**: Disaster recovery/backup costs?

## 2. Revenue Model & Financial Projections

### 2.1 Revenue Model
- **Q2.1.1**: How will the service generate revenue? (freemium, subscriptions, enterprise, usage-based)
- **Q2.1.2**: Pricing strategy? (cost-plus, value-based, competitive, freemium)
- **Q2.1.3**: Expected ARPU (Average Revenue Per User)? (by segment, tier)
- **Q2.1.4**: Free vs. paid split? (expected % of users)
- **Q2.1.5**: Pricing tiers? (Basic, Pro, Enterprise - prices)
- **Q2.1.6**: Expected conversion rate from free to paid?

### 2.2 Financial Projections (3-Year)
- **Q2.2.1**: Year 1 revenue target?
- **Q2.2.2**: Year 2 revenue target?
- **Q2.2.3**: Year 3 revenue target?
- **Q2.2.4**: Expected gross margin? (%)
- **Q2.2.5**: Expected operating margin? (%)
- **Q2.2.6**: Break-even timeline? (months to profitability)
- **Q2.2.7**: Profitability targets by year?

### 2.3 Unit Economics
- **Q2.3.1**: Expected CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)?
- **Q2.3.2**: Expected LTV (Customer Lifetime Value)?
- **Q2.3.3**: Expected LTV:CAC ratio? (3:1, 5:1, higher)
- **Q2.3.4**: Payback period for CAC? (months)
- **Q2.3.5**: Expected monthly churn rate? (% of customers)
- **Q2.3.6**: Expected annual retention rate? (%)
- **Q2.3.7**: Expected net retention/expansion rate? (% > 100%)

## 6. Financial Risk Management

### 6.1 Risks & Mitigation
- **Q6.1.1**: What financial risks are we concerned about?

  - Higher CAC than expected
  - Higher churn than expected
  - Higher infrastructure costs
  - Competitive pricing pressure
- **Q6.1.2**: Worst-case scenarios? (financial impact)
- **Q6.1.3**: Contingency budget? (% of total budget - 10%, 20%)
- **Q6.1.4**: Cost sensitivity? (what costs are flexible, what are fixed)
- **Q6.1.5**: Sensitivity analysis? (what if revenue is 20% lower, costs 20% higher)

### 6.2 Financial Controls
- **Q6.2.1**: Budget approval process? (thresholds for approval)
- **Q6.2.2**: Spending authorization limits? (who can approve what amount)
- **Q6.2.3**: Financial reporting frequency? (weekly, monthly, quarterly)

## Notes
- Align financial projections with business goals and market research
- Plan for contingencies and worst-case scenarios
- Monitor burn rate closely and adjust spending as needed
- Track unit economics carefully (CAC, LTV, churn)
- Regular financial reviews with leadership (monthly minimum)
