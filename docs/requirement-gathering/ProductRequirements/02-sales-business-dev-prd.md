# URL Shortener Service - Product Requirements Document (PRD)
## Sales/Business Development

## 1. Overview
This PRD defines Sales and Business Development requirements for the URL Shortener Service based on stakeholder interview inputs. It captures go-to-market strategy, pricing and monetization, channel and partnership plans, and sales execution metrics needed for launch and growth.

## 2. Goals
- Define clear target segments, personas, and competitive positioning.
- Establish launch motion and sales channel strategy by segment.
- Define pricing/packaging and monetization model with measurable assumptions.
- Align sales enablement assets, customer success model, and reporting cadence.
- Ensure sales growth targets are tied to retention and unit economics guardrails.

## 3. Scope
### In Scope
- ICP and persona definitions.
- Positioning, differentiation, and competitor response strategy.
- GTM, channel, and partner strategy.
- Pricing model, packages, and monetization options.
- Sales process, enablement artifacts, and CS handoff expectations.
- Sales KPIs, forecasting, and risk/mitigation planning.

### Out of Scope
- Final engineering implementation details.
- Corporate finance approvals and budget governance workflows.

## 4. Functional Requirements
### 4.1 Customer and Market Definition
- FR-SALES-001: Primary customer segments (SMB, mid-market, enterprise, creators/agencies) must be prioritized with entry criteria.
- FR-SALES-002: Buyer, influencer, and end-user personas must be defined by segment.
- FR-SALES-003: Initial geographic scope and expansion sequencing must be defined.
- FR-SALES-004: TAM assumptions and sizing approach must be documented.

### 4.2 Competitive Positioning
- FR-SALES-005: Top competitors and alternatives (build vs. buy) must be documented.
- FR-SALES-006: Feature/value comparison matrix must identify parity gaps and differentiators.
- FR-SALES-007: Segment-specific positioning statements and messaging pillars must be defined.
- FR-SALES-008: Win/loss hypotheses and objection-handling themes must be documented.

### 4.3 GTM and Channel Strategy
- FR-SALES-009: Launch approach (beta, phased, public) must include timeline and target cohorts.
- FR-SALES-010: Channel mix (self-serve, direct sales, partners/resellers) must be defined by segment.
- FR-SALES-011: Pipeline ownership across Marketing, Sales, and Customer Success must be defined.
- FR-SALES-012: Product-led growth assumptions (trial/freemium to paid conversion) must be specified.

### 4.4 Pricing and Monetization
- FR-SALES-013: Pricing model (tiered, usage-based, hybrid) must be defined and justified.
- FR-SALES-014: Package boundaries and feature entitlements per tier must be documented.
- FR-SALES-015: Enterprise pricing policy (volume discounts/commitments/custom terms) must be defined.
- FR-SALES-016: API and premium capability monetization rules must be documented.

### 4.5 Sales Enablement and Process
- FR-SALES-017: Required sales collateral (one-pagers, battlecards, ROI calculator, demos) must be defined.
- FR-SALES-018: Qualification framework and stage definitions must be documented.
- FR-SALES-019: Segment-specific sales cycle expectations and contract term norms must be defined.
- FR-SALES-020: Onboarding and success handoff flow must define ownership and SLA expectations.

### 4.6 Metrics and Risk Controls
- FR-SALES-021: Core metrics (pipeline, conversion, win rate, ACV, cycle time, churn, retention) must be defined.
- FR-SALES-022: Forecasting cadence and confidence thresholds must be documented.
- FR-SALES-023: Revenue targets by quarter and segment must be defined with assumptions.
- FR-SALES-024: Risk triggers (e.g., CAC overrun, conversion drop, churn spike) and mitigations must be documented.

## 5. Non-Functional Requirements
- NFR-SALES-001: All assumptions for pricing, growth, and conversion must be traceable and reviewable.
- NFR-SALES-002: Reporting definitions must be consistent across Sales, Product, and Finance.
- NFR-SALES-003: Segment strategy must be maintainable and updateable each planning cycle.
- NFR-SALES-004: Revenue operations data must support auditable decision-making.

## 6. Dependencies
- Product packaging and feature roadmap commitments.
- Operations support tiers and SLA definitions.
- Finance guardrails for discounting and CAC targets.
- Legal/compliance review for enterprise contracting and regional constraints.

## 7. Risks and Mitigations
- Risk: Weak differentiation versus incumbent tools.
  - Mitigation: Positioning by segment plus proof-oriented enablement materials.
- Risk: Poor free-to-paid conversion.
  - Mitigation: Package-value testing and trial onboarding optimization.
- Risk: Channel conflict between direct and partner motion.
  - Mitigation: Channel rules of engagement and deal-registration policy.
- Risk: Forecast inaccuracy for new segments.
  - Mitigation: Stage-based forecast confidence and monthly recalibration.

## 8. Acceptance Criteria
- A1: Target segments, positioning, and launch channel strategy are approved by Sales and Product stakeholders.
- A2: Pricing and package boundaries are documented with conversion and retention assumptions.
- A3: Sales process, enablement requirements, and CS handoff model are documented and approved.
- A4: Core sales KPI definitions and reporting cadence are defined and operational.
- A5: Major GTM risks and trigger-based mitigation plans are documented.
